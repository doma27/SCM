Inclinometers

Inclinometers are devices that measure angles relative to the horizontal. In the SCM, inclinometers are used to measure the angles of adjacent outer face rolls for caster alignment data. A body mounted inclinometer is used solely by the SCM software during data allocation analysis. The inclinometer sensors used for the alignment data are mounted on independently spring-loaded blades of sufficient length to lie on the tangent between the two consecutive rolls.

The body mounted inclinometer is fixed to a mounting bracket which is attached rigidly to the outer face of the SCM body. It is typically located near to the main electronics enclosure.

The sensor is powered from the regulated +12v DC supply. The output of the device varies according to the angle of the device. The sensors give a maximum output of +5v when its measuring axis is at +47 degrees and 0v when at -47 degrees

Generally the sensors are mounted at 45 degrees to give a measuring range of - 2 degrees to + 92 degrees (90 degrees is when the SCM is vertical).

The sensor output is electronically filtered before being fed to the single board computer (SBC) through the distribution blocks.
