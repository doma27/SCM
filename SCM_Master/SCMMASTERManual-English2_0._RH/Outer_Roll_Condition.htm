Outer Roll Condition

The outer roll condition results are obtained from the measured data of the outer face The face of the SCM that is in contact with the Outer Face Rolls, may also be referred to as fixed or back face. gap sensors as they detect the crest of the outer face rolls.

The extension of each sensor, at each roll position is measured so that the profile of the outer face roll can be determined. The measurement of each sensor has to be deducted from the outer face datum value, which compensates for the fact that the outer face sensors are recessed into the SCM body before the actual outer roll condition is known.

Figure 13: Diagram of Gap Sensor Sensors that are used to measure the roll gaps, and outer roll condition Readings

A series of gap sensors are arranged across the width of the SCM to indicate the deviation of any outer face roll from the horizontal as shown.

 

 

 

Figure 14: Diagram of Gap Sensor Readings

Positive values indicate roll wear, bend or roll bearing damage. Negative values on the extreme left and right of the roll length indicate a build-up of grease or debris on the roll surface.

Positive numbers completely across the width of the display could indicate that either the whole roll has collapsed or that the SCM has lifted off the outer roll possibly due to the inner roll being raised.
