    Calibration

    Connect the laptop computer to the SCM.

    Select the inclinometer calibration button on the laptop computer, from the calibration menu.

     

    1. Select the desired inclinometer to calibrate. This can be done by checking or un-checking the box above each inclinometer column . In a typical scenario, you would select all of them.

    2. If the rotary table has been set up correctly, as previously described, a half turn, anticlockwise, of the wheel should line up the number 2 on the Vernier scale with the zero on the rotary table scale.

    3. Power on the digital spirit level - the digital display should indicate -2°.

    4. Press the ENTER key on the laptop to take a reading for the -2° point.

    5. The display will automatically advance the highlight cursor to the next calibration value.

  14. Now rotate the wheel clockwise so that the 0 on the wheel scale, lines up with the zero on the rotary table scale. At this point the spirit level should read 0°. This should continue to 92° rotating the wheel clockwise, lining up the 0 or 2 on the wheel with the zero on the rotary table.

  15. Each half turn of the wheel will rotate the table 2°.

  16. Rotate the rotary table in steps of two degrees from -2° to 92° collecting data into the laptop computer memory at each step. This should be done using the Vernier scales as they are more accurate than the spirit level as the measurements move towards 92°.

    Because the digital spirit level can only read 0 to 90 degrees, 92° would be displayed as 88° when rotating in a clockwise direction.

    NOTE: To eliminate the effect of backlash / hysteresis always approach all the angular positions to be calibrated from the same clockwise direction of rotary travel.

    As the new calibration values are taken the data is temporarily saved on the laptop PC.

     

    In order to permanently save the data to the SCM it is necessary to select the calibration transfer button before exiting the calibration menu. Failure to do this will result in loss of the new calibration data.

     

    At the end of the calibration re-build the inclinometer assembly, ensuring that the blade moves smoothly on the springs and spacers.

    Partial Calibration

    If required a partial calibration can be performed by using the arrow keys to position the highlight cursor over the desired inclinometer and angle value.

    The rotary table should then be rotated to this angle using digital spirit level as reference and then press ENTER.

    Repeat this procedure for any other angles.

     

    When complete press the calibration transfer button to save the data.

     

    SERVICE AND MAINTENANCE

    ê~

    Important Warning Messages

    When maintaining the Spring Anvils and Roll Rotation units extreme care must be taken due to the high spring tensions involved.

    Always inspect the lifting lugs for signs of physical damage prior to performing any lifting operation of the SCM.

    Maintenance Schedule

    This schedule is a guide only and the frequency of maintenance is dependent on the frequency of use in the caster and should be decided by the customer but often enough to keep the SCM in good working order.

    Weekly Maintenance

    Ensure the charger is connected to the SCM and on charge.

    Check the battery voltage using the BATT menu selection.

    Ensure the SCM is clean after the last caster measurement.

    Check for any signs of physical damage to the SCM body and inclinometer measuring blades.

    Ensure the sensors are functioning using the check mode facility.

    Monthly Maintenance

    In addition to the weekly checks:

    • Calibrate the gap sensors.

    • Measure the SCM thickness.

    • Test the roll rotation sensors using the ROLL option.

    • Test the water spray sensors using the WATER option.

    • Check and tighten all glands and sensor connectors as necessary.

     

     

    Three Monthly Maintenance

    In addition to the weekly and monthly checks:

    • Calibrate the inclinometer sensors.

    • Spare batteries should be charged (using spare battery charging cable, supplied with spare batteries) or cycled through the SCM to keep them in good condition.

    • Inspect the lifting lugs for signs of physical damage and check that the securing bolts (if fitted) are tightened to 120Nm torque.

    • Remove gap sensors from their housings and clean using tissue paper or soft cotton cloth.

    Important Warning Messages

    The lifting lugs are secured to the SCM body using four Hex Socket Cap Screws M12 x 40 LG Grade 12.9 screws. The screws are tightened to a torque of 120Nm.  Should the screws be removed for any reason after being tightened they must be replaced with new screws and not re-used.

    Always inspect the lifting lugs for signs of physical damage prior to performing any lifting operation of the SCM. If in doubt get the opinion of a qualified lifting and handling engineer or contact Sarclad Limited for advice.

    Every four months check that the securing screws are sound and tightened to 120Nm torque.

    The accuracy of these devices permits the roll gap to be measured to an accuracy of +/- 0.1mm, which can be proved under calibration conditions.

    Gap Measuring Head Maintenance

    This procedure details dismantling and replacement of the assembly in case of regular maintenance or of failure:

    1. Remove the four screws securing the measuring head assembly to the SCM.

    2. Withdraw and dry the assembly and release the connector.

    3. Release and withdraw the gap sensor by removing the four screws.

    4. Clean the inside of the measuring head - do not grease

    Reconstruction is the reverse procedure to dismantling taking note of the precautions detailed in on the drawings.

    When replacing the assembly, make sure that the SCM thickness holes in the flange are in line with the hole in the flange seat on the SCM and that you can see a clear path through the SCM body.

    Ensure the longer stroke sensor is replaced on the inner face and that the shorter sensor is on the outer face.

    The sensor, which was extracted, should now be calibrated, followed by SCM thickness according to the calibration section.

    Gap Sensor Replacement

    Insert the sensor ensuring all parts of the measuring head are clean. Tighten the four retaining screws evenly.

    Replace the gap sensor into the SCM taking care to move cables in such a way as to leave a clear line of sight through the SCM thickness holes. Ensure the cables are out of the way any moving parts.

    Calibrate the sensor and measure the SCM thickness at the gap sensor position, which was removed.

    Inclinometers

    To measure the outer roll alignment and for data analysis functions, high precision, inclinometer sensors are used to produce an output proportional to the angle with respect to the horizontal.

    The accuracy of these devices permits the alignment angle to be measured to an accuracy of +/- 0.1 degrees (under calibration conditions).

    The sensor electronics are potted and cannot be dismantled.

    If the inclinometers are removed from their mounting position, they need to be re-fitted correctly in order to achieve good calibration.

    The inclinometer output is in the range of 0 – 5v and the voltage change depends on the angle of the device. This is converted into a digital value in the range of 0 – 32000.

     

    By adjusting the position of the inclinometer, the maximum and minimum values can be set to give the widest range of output values.

    The suggested way to set these sensors is to take a calibration measurement at -2° which should be between 50 and 200 counts.

    Another measurement should be taken at 92°. This reading should be <32000. The table on the previous page will give approximate values for both -2° and 92°.

    The values shown previously are not absolute, as they will vary between devices. The important part is the relative change in output between the angles and that both ends of the angular scale fall within the device’s maximum and minimum output values.

    These devices are set up during commissioning and should not need to be adjusted. However, if a device becomes faulty and has to be replaced, then the above procedure should be followed.

    Roll Rotation Sensors

    To measure the roll rotation, proximity sensors are used to produce a pulsed output proportional to the amount the sensor wheel is turned. A second proximity sensor detects when the sensor is on a roll.

    Figure 25: Proximity sensor

    The sensor electronics are fully potted and cannot be dismantled, only replaced.

    Figure 26: Location of Roll Ration sensors within SCM

    Roll Rotation Sensor Maintenance

    The roll rotation system requires no normal maintenance except for checking that there is no build-up of debris (grease, slag) around the spring and wheel cavity and that the sensor wheel freely rotates.

    Roll Rotation Sensor Replacement

    The following procedure gives details on how to dismantle and replace the proximity sensor assembly in case of failure.

    • Remove the sensor assembly by extracting the retaining screws.

    • Separate the connectors housed inside the SCM body.

    • Unscrew the locking nuts and remove the sensor.

    • Replacement of the assembly is a reverse procedure to the removal.

    • The position of the sensors should be adjusted to approximately 1.5mm from the surface of the sensor wheel (for rotation sensing), and the on roll sensor should switch off at approximately 5mm travel from initially going on a roll.
