Single Roll Out Of Position 

In the example, on the left Left when viewed from the mould in the casting direction. hand side, rolls 3-4-5 show a step error of 0.1 mm and a step error of +1.9 mm on the right Right when viewed from the mould in the casting direction. side. This means that if roll 4 were moved towards the centre of radius of the caster by these amounts, it would then be in the correct position relative to rolls 3 and 5.

In this example it should be noted that the step errors on the right hand side of -0.8 mm shown for rolls 2-3-4, and -0.9 mm shown for rolls 4-5-6 are a consequence of roll 4 being out of position. These will disappear when a correction to the position of roll 4 is made. This is a typical example of single roll being out of position within a caster segment and can be typically identified by an "x" value step error, preceded and followed by approx. "x/2" value step error of the opposite sign.
