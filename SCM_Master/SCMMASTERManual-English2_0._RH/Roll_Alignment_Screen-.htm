Roll Alignment Screen

Caster alignment data is divided into left side and right side caster measurement results. The data for each side is independent to the other.

The first column indicates the roll position from which an angle is measured to the next adjacent roll by the inclinometer blade.

The target angle for this measurement is indicated in the centre column.

The next columns then indicate the actual measured angles, the error in degrees between the target angle and the measured angle and the calculated millimetres of position error for each roll on the left and right sides of the caster.

The millimetres of position error, also referred to as step error, is a calculated value for a single roll, relative to its adjacent rolls.

A positive step error indicates that a roll is low; a negative step error indicates that a roll is high when compared to its neighbouring rolls.

The colour of the threshold boxes help to identify step errors that are above or below the acceptance thresholds.
