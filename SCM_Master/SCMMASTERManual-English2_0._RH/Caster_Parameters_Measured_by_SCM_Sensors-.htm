Caster Parameters Measured by SCM Sensors

The following caster parameters are measured with sensors housed in the SCM body. The actual measurements provided depend on the individual requirements of each customer.

ROLL GAP

The measurement relating to the distance between the outer face and inner face rolls.  Gap sensors on the inner and outer face of the SCM are used in pairs to determine the absolute distance between the rolls of the caster. Gap measurements are taken at a number of positions across the caster width.

OUTER ROLL CONDITION (ORC)

The profile of the outer face rolls across the width of the caster. Sensors on the outer face of the SCM are used to determine the deviation of the outer face rolls from a straight edge reference.

ROLL BEND

The bend or eccentricity of rotation of a pair of caster rolls. Gap sensors aligned vertically down the SCM body are used to measure the roll gap a number of times whilst the rolls are rotated. The difference between the maximum and minimum gap values gives an indication of the roll bend.

OUTER ROLL ALIGNMENT (ORA)

The angular alignment or curve of the caster radius. Measurements are taken using independently sprung blades to measure the angle between pairs of adjacent rolls to identify misplaced rolls or misaligned caster segments.

ROLL ROTATION

An indication of the freedom of rotation of each roll.

WATER SPRAY

The condition of the water spray cooling system is indicated by the relative amounts of water spray detected at a number of positions across the caster strand width.

TEMPERATURE

A measurement of the temperature within the SCM electronics enclosure is taken every minute.

BATTERY VOLTAGE

A measurement of the SCM battery voltage is taken every minute.

 

Data from each sensor is collected and stored by an on-board, battery powered computer, whilst the SCM is passed through the caster. At the end of the measurement, the data is transferred to a laptop PC where it is combined with calibration and caster parameters before displaying the actual analysed data.
