Gap Roll Layout Display

 

In this typical result display, the left-hand columns show the segment numbers, the roll numbers and the target gaps (as measured by a stick micrometer). The target gap values are the expected or desired gap measurements.

Next is displayed the roll configurations and then the results measured by the individual gap sensor Sensors that are used to measure the roll gaps, and outer roll condition pairs. The position of the data on the roll is representative of the actual location of where the measurements are taken.

The sensor numbers are identified at the top of the page and the result indicated is the deviation from target value, that is, difference between the measured gap and the target gap.

If the measured gap is greater than the target gap, the display shows an upward bar graph and the numerical error result is positive. If the measured gap is less than the target gap, the display shows a downward bar graph and the numerical error result is negative.

The colour of the bar graph depends on the acceptance thresholds values. Results within threshold are shown in green, results above the upper threshold level are shown in red and results below the lower threshold level are shown in blue. Typical acceptance levels are +/- 0.5mm

Driven rolls are indicated by solid bars extending from the roll as shown. Bearing lanes where no gap can be measured are indicated by a X.
