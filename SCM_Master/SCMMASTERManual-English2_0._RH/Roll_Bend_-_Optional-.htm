Roll Bend - Optional

The roll bend measurement is based on the principle that a rotating roll pair (inner and outer) will present a different gap to the measurement system when the SCM is passed by the turning roll, if there is bend present. If readings are taken at different 'roll turned' positions, an indication of possible bend will result.

The SCM takes three readings for each roll using the gap sensors positioned down the length of the SCM.

Figure 15: Method for taking roll bend measurements

 

The difference between these three readings is used to indicate the roll bend. This calculation results in an indication of bend.

As the measuring head is pulled through the casting machine, the spring anvils impose a load on the rolls sufficient to cause the rolls to rotate. The roll gap is measured by identical sensors at positions A, B and C on the measuring head. Roll bend is indicated by the variation in gap readings at A, B and C.

  • Consider gap at position 'A' = 220.5mm

  • Consider gap at position 'B' = 220.0mm

  • Consider gap at position 'C' = 220.3mm

The results shown give an indication of bend present within the pair of rolls shown equivalent to 0.5mm.

The result will indicate which pair of rolls is bent, but further visual examination is required to isolate the bent roll.
