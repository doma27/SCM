Step Error Calculation

To calculate the step error of a roll it is necessary to measure the angle of that roll in relation to its adjacent rolls so that the actual and expected values can be compared and the error identified. From this data, the step error can be then be calculated.

If roll 2 is displaced downwards then A1 will increase and A2 will decrease, the difference between these two angles gives dA, which is then compared to the expected theoretical angle.

The difference then needs to be divided by 2 to give the angular error of the roll.

Then by using trigonometry and the mean tangential pitch of the roll from the caster details the roll displacement can be calculated.
