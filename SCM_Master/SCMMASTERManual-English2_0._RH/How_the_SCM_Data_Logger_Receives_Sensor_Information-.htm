How the SCM Data Logger Receives Sensor Information

The analogue output signals from the Gap, Inclinometer and Water Spray sensors are filtered and processed by the single board computer before being processed by the internal analogue to digital (A to D) converters. The A to D converters convert the 0 to 10 V DC analogue signal outputs from the signal conditioning units to a 16-bit digital signal represented by a value from 0 to 32000.

The digital output signals from the roll rotation and roll detection sensors are connected to the data logger digital input channels via the M8 distribution blocks. The digital inputs to the single board computer (SBC) are 0 or 5 volt dc levels that represent either logic 0 or logic 1 data.

The SBC constantly reads the outputs from the A to D converters and digital input channels. The received information is then processed and written to the SDHC memory card which can then be downloaded to the laptop computer for analysis.
