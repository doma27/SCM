Outer Roll Condition Roll Layout Screen

This screen shows the condition of the outer face The face of the SCM that is in contact with the Outer Face Rolls, may also be referred to as fixed or back face. rolls when compared to a straight edge placed across the caster width.

The individual deviation readings are shown on the roll configurations. The position of the data on the roll is representative of the actual location of where the measurements are taken.

The sensor numbers are identified at the top of the page and the results indicated are the deviations from a straight edge reference, which is provided by the SCM body.

The maximum deviation across the measurements for each roll is calculated and displayed next to the roll number.

The target value for this display is 0mm of deviation.

If the reading is positive the display shows a downward bar graph and this could indicate a roll condition such as wear or a collapsed bearing.

If the reading is negative the display will show an upward bar graph and this could indicate a roll condition such as a build-up of slag, scale or debris on the roll surface.

The colour of the bar graph depends on the acceptance thresholds values. Results within threshold are shown in green and results above the threshold level are shown in red.

This data can also be viewed in the threshold summary format, where a visual over-view of the outer roll condition with reference to the tolerance threshold limit, is provided.
