Gap Error Display Results

You can extract the following data from roll gap error results after measurement:

§  Results within the acceptance threshold are in the correct position and can be normally ignored by the maintenance engineers.

§  If a single roll gap shows a positive result, then the gap at that position is wide indicating that either the inner, outer or the pair of rolls is out of position. Looking at the outer roll alignment and outer roll condition results will assist in showing which case is the most probable.

§  If all the rolls in a segment are showing a positive result, then the inner segment may be loose, incorrect shims under the segment supports, trapped dirt under the supports or the hydraulic gap adjustment may be wrong.

§  If all the rolls in a segment show a negative result then the segment is narrow and may have supports loaded too tightly (hydraulically or mechanically).

§  A gradual degradation of results across the caster with full-length rolls indicates an end-bearing problem.

Large errors at the ends of the split roll positions indicate the possibility of bearing problems at that position.

§  No Log could indicate a roll so far out of position that a sensor does not touch it. This may be due to a collapsed bearing, gaps being too far out of target or the inner part of the segment not in the correct position. It could also indicate that the SCM is running off the centre line of the caster, and the gaps are passing over bearing lanes.

§  If a result column at a sensor pair is always in error by a constant amount throughout the caster, the SCM thickness measurement should be repeated, and the measurement re-loaded into the laptop or PC computer before conclusions about results are made.

§  An X indicates that the sensor is travelling over a bearing and cannot take a valid measurement.

Roll gap results should be used in conjunction with outer roll alignment and outer roll condition to give more information, especially to determine whether problems are on the inner or outer face The face of the SCM that is in contact with the Outer Face Rolls, may also be referred to as fixed or back face. rolls.
