Important Warning Messages

It is recommended that adjustments of outer roll alignment be executed with great care due to the severe consequences involved.

For all single roll errors it is recommended that the largest error should be investigated first and adjusted to approx. 50% of the error figure, as the adjacent rolls may not be in their ideal position and so could influence the necessary adjustment required.

A repeat measurement would then give an indication of the level of improvement.

If analysis shows segment step errors at the segment boundary it is recommended that in these circumstances the error should be investigated by identifying whether the upper or lower of the two segments is in error and the appropriate segment be adjusted by 100% of the error figure. This adjustment should halve the step error shown. Alternatively, if it is not clear which one of the two segments is out of position, then the correction may be shared between the two segment ends moving each in opposite directions.

For results in the case of segment angular misalignment it is recommended that segments either side of the angular error are analysed in order to determine whether the top or bottom of the segment is to be adjusted.

It should be noted that the adjacent segment interfaces to the one being adjusted will be altered as a direct consequence of this segment correction.

A repeat measurement would then give an indication of the level of improvement.
