Bleeding the Hydraulic System

This section describes the bleeding and set up of the system in case of problems or failure.

  • All components should be installed as per drawings.

  • Stand the SCM vertical in its stand and remove the cover plates.

  • Connect the feed hose from the trolley mounted HPU to the SCM via the quick release connection point.

  • Locate the pressure relief valve on the trolley mounted HPU and screw out the pressure adjusting screw to its outermost position (lowest pressure setting).

  • Using the Infra-Red transmitter, press the HYDRAULICS ON button to energise the pressure holding solenoid valve. The flashing red LED should illuminate on the SCM interface units.

  • Actuate the valve on the trolley mounted HPU for approx. 10 seconds.

  • 'Bleed' out the air from the system at each cylinder connection in turn. It may be necessary to re-activate the hand valve (for a few seconds only) to inject more oil into the system and consequently more oil will be required to keep the HPU reservoir full.

  • When all air has been 'bled' from the system, all connection points should be tightened prior to final set-up of the system.

  • Connect the maintenance pressure gauge to the gauge point.

  • Ensure the pressure holding solenoid is activated (flashing red LED illuminated on the interface units).

  • Screw in the pressure adjusting screw on the pressure relief valve by one turn at a time, and run the HPU (by actuating the hand valve) until the spring anvils are fully retracted and note the pressure reading.

  • Adjust the pressure setting on the pressure switch at approximately 5 bar above the anvil retracted pressure and ensure the static red LED on the interface units is active.

  • The pressure relief valve should now be set to 20 bar above the anvil retract pressure and the adjusting screw locked off.

  • The reservoir should now be filled with oil to the fluid level on the indicator on the HPU.

  • Using the IR transmitter, the pressure holding solenoid valve should be de-energised by operating the HYDRAULICS OFF key. The flashing red LED and the static red LED should go out.

  • The spring anvils will extend out to their normal positions and thus extend the hydraulic cylinders.

  • Repeat the anvil retract / release procedure several times to ensure correct operation and also check the anvils will remain retracted for at least 1 hour.

  • Remove the maintenance pressure gauge and fit the cap to the gauge point.
