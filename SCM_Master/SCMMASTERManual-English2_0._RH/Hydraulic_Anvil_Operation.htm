Important Warning Messages

Ensure the Anvils are fully retracted beneath the hard profile of the SCM body before attempting insertion into the mould.

Ensure the hose is disconnected prior to moving the chain and SCM.

Heavy weights are involved in this procedure. Take due care when moving the SCM. At least two operators are required

The power drain on the hydraulic solenoid decreases the measurement time capacity by approximately 1.5 hours for every hour it is activated. for this reason, the solenoid should be switched on as late as possible prior to a measurement run and deactivated as soon as possible once the SCM is in the correct start position.

Transport the mobile hydraulic pump to the close proximity to the SCM.

Connect the pump power lead to an appropriate 240V mains supply.

Connect the hydraulic power unit (HPU) hose to the SCM via the quick release connector ensuring both the male and female halves are free from dirt.

Switch on the pump by pressing the green button and allow the motor to run up to speed.

On the IR transmitter, press the ENERGISE hydraulics button  to energise the pressure holding solenoid on the SCM hydraulic unit. The flashing red LED should illuminate on the SCM interface unit.

The static LED indicates that the target hydraulic pressure has been reached.

Activate the sprung offset valve lever on the HPU to retract the spring anvils on the SCM.

When the anvils are fully depressed flush or 1mm below the SCM profile and the red static LED is lit.

The sprung offset valve lever on the HPU should be released to its original position.

The feed hose should now be disconnected from the SCM via the quick release connectors.

The HPU should now be stopped (red button) and disconnected from the electrical supply.

The SCM should now be passed through the mould.

After the SCM is clear of the mould, release the anvils by clicking the DE‑ENERGISE hydraulics button .

Once the anvils have been released the flashing and the static hydraulic LEDs will turn off.
