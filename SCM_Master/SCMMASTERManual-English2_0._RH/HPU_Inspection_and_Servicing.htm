HPU Inspection and Servicing

§  To keep the HPU system in good condition, regular visual inspections are important. Initially these inspections should be made every day to minimise leaks, build-up of dirt and keep fins clean to maintain cooling.

§  Check the fluid level on the gauge. Ensure it is not below the minimum mark to avoid pump failure.

§  Fluid temperature should be checked (normally 20 - 70 degrees Celsius) to minimise damage to hoses and seals or reducing the oil viscosity, which could lead to shorter pump life.

§  The condition of the return line filter should be checked at the same interval as the oil level noting the reading on the indicator.

§  The filler breather cap should be removed for visual inspection of any sign of contamination.

§  The gear pump is not a serviceable item other than the seal kits and rear plates. If the pump will not maintain pressure and the seals are intact, then the whole pump will need replacing.

§  A relief valve, which usually has been pre-set and tested, controls the pressure of the system - the figure can altered by adjustment of the relief valve.

§  To adjust the relief valve, loosen the lock nut, keeping the central grub screw in place.

§  To reduce the pressure, turn the central screw anti-clockwise and with the HPU running, read the pressure on the gauge.

§  To increase the pressure, turn the central screw clockwise, however this must be carried out constantly checking the motor current does not exceed the rating shown on the label.

§  Once the required pressure has been attained, lock the grub screw in position with the lock nut.
