Gap Sensors

Figure 2: Gap sensors

Gap sensors are used to measure the roll gaps, and outer roll condition.

Each gap sensor Sensors that are used to measure the roll gaps, and outer roll condition is a sealed, spring loaded unit fitted inside a stainless steel measuring head.

As the tip of the sensor is pressed in against the spring force, the output of the device varies linearly from 5v to 0v (nominally), proportional to the movement.

The sensor is powered by a regulated dc supply and has a measuring stroke appropriate to the housing into which the sensor is fitted.  The inner face The face of the SCM that is in contact with the Inner Face Rolls, may also be referred to as the loose or adjustable face. sensor travel is typically longer than the outer face The face of the SCM that is in contact with the Outer Face Rolls, may also be referred to as fixed or back face. travel due to spring anvils on the inner face pushing the SCM hard against the outer face of the caster, so creating a longer range over which the inner sensors must measure.

The sensor output is fed to the single board computer (SBC Single-board computer) through the distribution blocks.
