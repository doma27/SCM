Caster Segment Step Misalignment

Another type of error typically identified is that of a step misalignment between segments. An example of this is displayed below.

At segment 1 and 2 interface i.e. between rolls 9 and 1, where a step error of 1.7 mm for rolls 8-9-1 and -1.4 mm for rolls 9-1-2 are indicated.

This type of error can typically be identified by a step error occurring for the last roll in a segment and a step error of the opposite sign occurring for the first roll in the next segment.

Rectification of this misalignment can be achieved by identifying the segment in error and then adjusting the segment cylinders by the sum of the errors across the segment boundary.

The presence of a large step error at the entry and exit of a segment usually identifies the segment in error. If segment 2 is found to be at fault then the bottom of the segment should be adjusted away from the inner segment by approximately 2.1mm.
