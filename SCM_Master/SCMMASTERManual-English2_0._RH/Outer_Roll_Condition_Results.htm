Outer Roll Condition Results

The following inferences can be made from outer roll condition results:

§  If all the values are at or near zero, the outer face The face of the SCM that is in contact with the Outer Face Rolls, may also be referred to as fixed or back face. rolls are in a straight line across the caster width.

§  If readings are positive this could be due to roll wear or a bearing problem.

§  If readings are negative this could be due to the build-up of deposits such as debris and scale on the caster rolls.

§  If all results across the roll show a large positive reading, then the SCM may have been displaced (lifted) off the outer rolls Identified as the rolls in the caster whose radius is greater than that of the Inner face rolls (or “fixed side”)..

Investigation of the gap error and outer roll alignment results will enable the operator to diagnose the problems in greater detail.
