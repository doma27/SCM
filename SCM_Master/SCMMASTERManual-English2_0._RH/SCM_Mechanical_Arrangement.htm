SCM Mechanical Arrangement

Mounted into the SCM body are the individual sensors used for the caster measurements and internally mounted are the system electronics, batteries and hydraulics if required.

The gap sensors are mounted into the SCM body below the hard profile 'pass line' to guard against damage.

The inclinometer Devices that measure angles relative to the horizontal sensors (used for angle measurement) are attached to blades that are spring mounted and contact the roll surface independently of the SCM position.

The roll rotation sensors are mounted on both faces of the SCM body and again are sprung loaded.

Spring anvils are built into the SCM to fulfil several functions:

This ensures the SCM travels in a stable manner along the outer face rolls of the caster and enables the gaps and outer face roll angles and condition to be accurately measured.

The spring anvil frictional force achieves rotation of the caster rolls to enable roll freedom detection by the roll rotation sensors as the SCM is transported through the caster.

Displace the heaviest rolls in the caster to help identify collapsed roll bearings or damaged mountings.

A specially designed intermediate link arrangement with the same connection characteristics as the dummy bar head is mounted to the bottom end of the SCM. The link has an anti-rotation feature so that the SCM cannot rotate beyond the horizontal, but it does allow the SCM to rotate to the vertical position. The SCM and link are fitted to the customers chain in place of the dummy bar head during a caster measurement. The link also has a sliding bush arrangement to allow the SCM to be forced hard on to the outer face rolls and to allow the SCM to remain independent of any irregular dummy bar chain movements.

Lifting lugs are fitted to the SCM to allow for ease of transportation.
